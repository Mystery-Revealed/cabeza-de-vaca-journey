# "Cabeza de Vaca's Journey" — Build Specification
### Unit 2 Game · Age of Contact · *Common Build Standards apply (Pattern A — Engine Game)*

## 1. At a Glance
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **TEKS** | 7.2B (Cabeza de Vaca, exploration), 7.2A (peoples he lived among), 7.9A |
| **Role** | Álvar Núñez Cabeza de Vaca, 1528–1536 (everyone plays him — one class-wide group) |
| **Type** | Solo survival journey, choice-based — 6 chapters × 2 decisions = **12 graded actions** |
| **Adapter** | `cabezaDeVaca.js` · meters `{ health, standing, hope }` start 50 — **standing** = trust with the Native peoples he depends on |

**Pitch:** Shipwrecked on a Texas island with nothing — not even shoes — you must survive eight years among peoples whose languages you don't speak, becoming a slave, a trader, a healer, and finally a legend who walks all the way to Mexico.

**Teaching idea:** the first European account of Texas is a story where the explorer survives only by **learning from and depending on Native peoples** — flipping the conqueror script. His report later urged humane treatment (and accidentally fueled gold rumors that launched more expeditions). Alongside him walks **Estevanico**, the enslaved Moroccan-born man who became one of the great explorers of the continent — name him and give him his due.

## 2. Content Bank
1527 Narváez expedition disaster → ~90 castaways on rafts wreck near **Galveston Island, November 1528** ("Isla de Malhado," the Island of Misfortune) → winter starvation and sickness; local Karankawa-area peoples share food → years of servitude and hardship → Cabeza de Vaca becomes a **trader** carrying goods between coastal and inland peoples → with **Estevanico, Dorantes, and Castillo**, escapes west (1534) → the four gain fame as **healers**; thousands escort them village to village → through the lands of the **Jumano** and mountain-and-basin country → meet Spanish slavers near the Pacific coast (1536) — and Cabeza de Vaca is horrified by them → his account, *La Relación*, describes Texas's lands and peoples and argues for kindness; garbled retellings feed the **Cities of Gold** rumors → Coronado and others follow.

## 3. Mechanics
Meters: **Health** ❤️, **Standing** 🤝 (trust of the peoples he lives among — the game's engine), **Hope** 🌅. Ending = sum → tiers ("The Long Walk Home" / "Survivor of Malhado" / "Lost to the Island") + debrief: eight years, four survivors of ninety, a book that changed how some Spaniards argued Native peoples should be treated — and rumors that launched the next wave of expeditions (7.2B's cause-and-effect).

## 4. Sample Scripted Phase + Beat Matrix
**Chapter 1 — Malhado (decision 1):** *Your raft breaks apart in November surf. Local people find your freezing party on the beach.*
- ✅ **Accept their help humbly — food, fire, shelter.** Health +15, Standing +10. *"They wept at the castaways' suffering and shared what they had. Pride would have been a grave."*
- ⚠️ **Accept, but keep the group apart and guarded.** Health +5, Standing −5. *"Warmth with walls. You'll need these people far more than they need you."*
- ❌ **Demand help in the name of Spain.** Standing −15. *"You are a starving stranger with no shoes. Spain is very far away."*

**Chapter 1 (decision 2):** *Winter deepens; food is scarce for everyone.* ✅ Work as the band works — dig roots, haul wood, earn your keep (Standing +10, Health +5) · ⚠️ Trade your last Spanish goods for food (Hope +5) · ❌ Steal from the stores (Standing −15).

**Beat matrix:**
| Ch | Beat | Right answer teaches |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | Years of servitude; brutal labor | Endure, learn the languages, watch and learn the land (survival = learning) |
| 3 | The trader years | Carry shells inland, hides back — become useful between peoples (his real niche) |
| 4 | The escape west; the four reunite | Go together — Estevanico's gifts for languages lead the way (name him ✅) |
| 5 | The healer's road | Treat the sick humbly, take no payment but food, credit the people's own faith (his account's voice); thousands escort them |
| 6 | Meeting the Spanish slavers | Stand between the slavers and your escorts; protect the people who carried you (his real, documented stand) — then tell the truth in your report |

## 6. Command Center & Notes
Class-wide accuracy; PDF footer TEKS 7.2B, 7.2A. **Sensitivity:** Native peoples are rescuers, teachers, and hosts with full agency — the hardship years are told without villainizing any people ("hard years among hard-pressed peoples"). **Estevanico is named and honored.** The slaver scene is the moral spine — keep it, at headline level. His report's double legacy (humane argument + gold rumors) is the debrief's closing thought.
